Our Services



Pediatrics



Pediatrics is a medical department dedicated to children's health. Pediatricians diagnose, treat, and prevent childhood illnesses, developmental issues, and injuries. They provide vaccinations, monitor growth and development, offer guidance to parents, and ensure the well-being of infants, children, and adolescents, promoting their physical and emotional health.

Radiology

Radiology is a medical department specializing in diagnostic imaging. Radiologists use techniques like X-rays, CT scans, MRIs, and ultrasounds to visualize internal structures, aiding in the diagnosis of various medical conditions. They interpret images to guide treatment decisions, detect diseases, assess injuries, and monitor progress, contributing crucial information to patient care.

Cardiology

Cardiology is the medical specialty focused on the heart and circulatory system. Cardiologists diagnose and treat heart-related conditions such as coronary artery disease, heart failure, and arrhythmias. They employ tests like EKGs, echocardiograms, and catheterization to assess heart health, provide treatment plans, and perform procedures like angioplasty or stent placement to improve heart function.

Oncology

Doctors are medical professionals. They are qualified and trained health care professionals who diagnose and treat various health conditions in human beings. There are various branches of medicine. Doctors specialize in studying and treating diseases of the different parts of the body. For example, cardiologists, ophthalmologists, dentists, neurologists and dermatologists are specialists in treating diseases of specific parts of the human body.

Psychiatry

Psychiatry is a medical specialty that focuses on mental health and emotional well-being. Psychiatrists diagnose and treat mental illnesses, including depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, and bipolar disorder. They employ therapy, medication, and counseling to help patients manage symptoms, improve mental health, and enhance overall psychological functioning, providing crucial support for mental wellness.

Emergency Department

The Emergency Department (ED) is a critical part of a hospital that provides immediate medical care for acute and life-threatening conditions. ED staff, including doctors and nurses, assess patients, stabilize injuries, and administer urgent treatments. They also coordinate with other hospital departments for specialized care, ensuring rapid and efficient emergency medical attention.
